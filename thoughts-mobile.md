### [Title of the Observation]
**:** [Brightness control as a one-way transform]

**The problem:** [There are times a fully-blank screen is good -- it saves battery, it's unobtrusive in a dark place, it doesn't call attention to itself generally. But in a brightly lit area, even a screen that's merely dim can be hard to see at all. If it's locked, it's hard to enter a PIN; even if it's not, it'seven more annoying: It's hard to see the brightness controls themselves in order to fix the problem. A glitch of this kind drove me batty a few months ago, and I had to blindly press the position of the number keys until I got the PIN right.]

**The fix** [Employ the phone's physical buttons, or add a button / toggle for this purpose. Or use a squeeze sensor to smoothly, temporarily raise the screen brightness. On Pixel phones (Pixel 6 and newer), Power + Volume Up by default activates the Power controls menu; if that would also force increased brightness and also give the user access to the brightness slider, the problem is solved. Those Power controls are pretty useless if you can't see the screen.]

### [Title of the Observation]
**:** [Keep the swiping keyboard small.]

**The problem:** [Swiping keyboards are great. So are adequately large on-screen interface elements. But swiping is best and fastest when the keyboard itself is kept as small as a user wants it. A bigger keyboard just means a longer, slower path.]

**The fix** [Even if it's a large tablet, rather than a tiny phone, at least give the option of a phone-sized swiping keyboard.]
