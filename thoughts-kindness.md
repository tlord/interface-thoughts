### [Title of the Observation]
**:** [Busy signal means "broken.""]

If someone calls a business or a government office, it's because they need an answer -- and either for some reason they can't go online to get the information they need, or they tried it that way already without success. If you hang up on them, now they'll be angry as well as frustrated.

For decades now, the answer is to never give a busy signal at all. The office is closed? That's fine. Play a message with options. Play short-form urgent messages on a loop. Let the user leave a message, and (honestly) promise to call them back. Tell the user a number they can text to instead, or an email address. Offer AI-driven responses by text. Give a voice-based search option. (Even a poor IVR is right twice a day.)
