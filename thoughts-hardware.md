### Get the weight right -- and lighter isn't always better

**The Principle:** Control surfaces should provide "Weight" proportional to the consequence of the action.


**The Friction:** 

Modern "drive-by-wire" systems and capacitive touch buttons on steering wheels remove the physical resistance that tells a driver's brain how much input they are giving. When a steering wheel feels like a video game controller, the "interface" between man and machine loses its high-fidelity feedback loop. An elelectronic keyboard with a bland spring action might feel like a pipe organ, but never like a piano.

**The Resolution:** 

Return to physical, weighted resistance for critical inputs. A steering wheel should fight back just enough to remind you that you are moving two tons of metal. A keyboard or a trackpad should move easily, but require active intent.
